Scrum Roles

The product owner is responsible for maximizing the developments (return of investment). The vision for the product is theirs, and they are held responsible. They are constantly re-prioritizing the Product Backlog, and settings long-term plans such as release dates. They have the end decision on whether to ship or continue development. The Product Owner considers the stakeholders interests and has the ‘leadership’ role.

The team consists of ideally 7 (two above or below) cross-functional members (includes people with and without developing skills, such as business analysts). They are self-managing, without any hierarchy or assigned roles. They work in sprints and discuss developments with the Product Owner. It is important that all members are available and work together (preferably in one room).

The scrum master facilitates the process and helps resolve any obstacles. They create the space and environment for the Development Team to self-organize and manage. They make sure the team is not distracted and keeps focused. The Scrum master enforces the time boxes and makes sure the Scrum Process is clear and followed. Even though the Scrum Master facilitates the Scrum meetings, they do not have any special decision-making authority or management authority over the team.
