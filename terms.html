The terms of Scrum

Agile development:
cross-functional team empowered to make decisions, focusing on rapid iteration, with continuous customer input along the way.

Product Backlog:
the lifetime of the product, continuously updated by the Product Owner to reflect changes that the customer wants such as new ideas an insight but also the obstacles.A product backlog is the refined prioritized lists of features needed. It is a list of all the things that could be done by the team, set in the order of their priority.

Scrum:
an agile development method/framework, meaning it emphasizes on work in progress rather than writing up front and fixing later.

Sprints:
the set period in which certain tasks will be completed.

Stakeholder:
Someone who is invlovled and interested in the project through for example investments or being affected by it.

Velocity:
The Team tracks how much work can be done in each Sprint, with this information a final project release date can be set based on their average called velocity.

Cross-functional team:
a team which includes people with and without developing skills, such as business analysts.

Definition of Done:
an established measure that all items must meet, such as, ‘coded to standard, reviewed and tested, integrated and documented’ to make sure an item is fully finished.

Sprint Burndown Chart:
a chart that shows how many hours are left that separate the team from the goal. Each day a new estimation of how many hours of work remain until the Team’s tasks are finished.

Estimated Work Remaining:
the number of hours that a team member estimates they will need to complete tasks. This is tracked every day, and the total estimated hours remaining will be counted together.

Sprint Review Meeting:
an around four hour meeting at the end of every sprint where the Team and Product Owner come together with the stakeholders to explain what happened in the sprint, demonstrating what has happened so far and the findings that they have, and plans for what would be best to do next.

All information about Scrum has been gathered from: Deemer, P., Benefield, G., Larman, C., & Vodde, B. (2010). The Scrum Primer (Version 1.2). Retrieved from http://www.goodagile.com/scrumprimer/scrumprimer.pdf
