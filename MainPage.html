What is Agile Scrum?

Scrum is an agile development method/framework, meaning it emphasizes on work in progress rather than writing up front and fixing later. There are three roles in the Scrum method: The Product owner, Scrum Development Team and the ScrumMaster. When a team uses Scrum, they work together as a cross-functional group, making decisions together instead of having strict functions or hierarchies.

Scrum structures a development into sections, these are Sprints. These sprints cannot take longer than a month and cannot have breaks in-between. Sprints have to start and end on a set date, whether the work is done or not. When starting with a sprint, the team picks 5 items from a prioritized list, and spends the sprint working on them, they cannot change within a sprint. During the sprint the team discusses the progress daily. At the end of the sprint, this progress is presented to the stakeholders and they will give feedback that is then incorporated into the next sprint. The main theme for Scrum is ‘inspect and adapt’. In the first step of Scrum, the Product owner creates the product vision, this will then become the refined and prioritized list of features called: The Product Backlog. This is the road map for the product.

The backlog is everything that can be done by the team, in order of priority. The Product Backlog is always being updated by the product owner to show the changes in the needs of the customers and other perceived developments (further explained below). This way the ROI (return of investment) is maximized. Whilst developing, the Team tracks how much work can be done in each Sprint, with this information a final project release date can be set based on their average called velocity.
